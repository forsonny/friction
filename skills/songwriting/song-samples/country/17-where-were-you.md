# Where Were You (When the World Stopped Turning)

**Artist:** Alan Jackson
**Album:** Drive (2002)
**Genre:** Country

---

## Structure Analysis

**Structure Type:** C (Slow Burn)

| Section | Lines | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Verse 1 | 12 | The questions |
| Chorus | 8 | The uncertainty |
| Verse 2 | 12 | Reactions |
| Chorus | 8 | Repeat |
| Bridge | 8 | The faith |
| Final Chorus | 8 | Resolution |

**Why this structure works:**
Long verses allow for accumulated questions. The chorus is the human condition in crisis. Slow build matches processing grief.

---

## Opening Technique

**Technique:** 4 (Rhetorical Question)

**Opening line(s):**
> "Where were you when the world stopped turning on that September day"

**Why it works:**
The question everyone asked. "World stopped turning" is specific to the event. We're immediately in shared trauma.

---

## Hook Analysis

**The hook:**
> "Where were you when the world stopped turning"

**Syllable count:** 10

**Conversational test:** Pass - It's literally what people asked each other

**Why it works:**
- Universal question after 9/11
- "World stopped turning" = unique phrase for unique event
- Question format invites participation
- Doesn't mention 9/11 directly - trusts listener

---

## Notable Lyrical Techniques

### 1. List as structure
**Example:**
> Multiple "did you" questions

**Why it works:** Shows range of reactions without judging

### 2. Avoiding politics
**Example:**
> "I'm just a singer of simple songs"

**Why it works:** Acknowledges complexity without taking sides

### 3. Faith through action
**Example:**
> "Turn off that violent old movie"

**Why it works:** Shows, doesn't preach

---

## "Said Not Written" Lines

Lines that sound like real speech:

1. > "I'm just a singer of simple songs"
   - Sounds said because: Self-deprecation, disclaiming authority

2. > "Did you turn off that violent old movie you're watching"
   - Sounds said because: Specific domestic response

3. > "Did you go out and buy a gun"
   - Sounds said because: Blunt question about real reaction

---

## Cliche Avoidance

**Cliches this song could have used but didn't:**

| Expected Cliche | What They Did Instead |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| "God bless America" | Questioned faith, didn't assert it |
| "United we stand" | Showed different reactions, unity not assumed |
| "Heroes" framing | Showed ordinary reactions |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. "Where were you" works for generational moment songs
2. Question lists show range without judgment
3. Admitting your limitations as writer builds trust
