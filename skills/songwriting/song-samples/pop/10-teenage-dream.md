# Teenage Dream

**Artist:** Katy Perry
**Album:** Teenage Dream (2010)
**Genre:** Power pop

---

## Structure Analysis

**Structure Type:** B (Chorus First... builds to it)

| Section | Lines | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Verse 1 | 8 | The escape |
| Pre-Chorus | 4 | Build |
| Chorus | 8 | The thesis |
| Verse 2 | 8 | Deeper |
| Pre-Chorus | 4 | Build |
| Chorus | 8 | Repeat |
| Bridge | 4 | "Imma get your heart racing" |
| Final Chorus | 8 | Victory lap |

**Why this structure works:**
Standard pop done well. Pre-chorus builds anticipation, chorus delivers euphoria.

---

## Opening Technique

**Technique:** 3 (Action in Progress)

**Opening line(s):**
> "You think I'm pretty without any makeup on"

**Why it works:**
Specific intimacy detail. "Without any makeup" = vulnerable, real. Reported observation.

---

## Hook Analysis

**The hook:**
> "You make me feel like I'm living a teenage dream"

**Syllable count:** 13

**Conversational test:** Pass - How someone describes intense love

**Why it works:**
- "Make me feel like" acknowledges the fantasy
- "Teenage dream" = idealized, intense, not-quite-real
- Nostalgia AND present combined

---

## Notable Lyrical Techniques

### 1. Physical intimacy specifics
**Example:**
> "Let you put your hands on me in my skin tight jeans"

**Why it works:** Sensory, specific, not abstract

### 2. Duality of real and fantasy
**Example:**
> "Feel like" = acknowledging it's a feeling

**Why it works:** Grounded in perception, not statement

### 3. Youth as state of mind
**Example:**
> "Teenage dream" isn't literal teenage

**Why it works:** Metaphor for intensity of first love

## "Said Not Written" Lines

Lines that sound like real speech:

1. > "You think I'm funny when I tell the punchline wrong"
   - Sounds said because: Specific moment of insecurity

2. > "Let's go all the way tonight"
   - Sounds said because: Direct proposition

3. > "My heart stops when you look at me"
   - Sounds said because: Hyperbole we use in speech

## Cliche Avoidance

**Cliches this song could have used but didn't:**

| Expected Cliche | What They Did Instead |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| "You're perfect to me" | Specific: thinks she's pretty without makeup |
| "I love you" | "You make me feel" - about the effect |
| "Fairy tale romance" | "Teenage dream" - more specific fantasy |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. "Without makeup" is more intimate than "as you are"
2. "Feel like" is more honest than "am"
3. Specific physical details ground fantasy
