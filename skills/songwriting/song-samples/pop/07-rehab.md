# Rehab

**Artist:** Amy Winehouse
**Album:** Back to Black (2006)
**Genre:** Soul-pop

---

## Structure Analysis

**Structure Type:** E (No Bridge)

| Section | Lines | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Verse 1 | 8 | The intervention |
| Chorus | 8 | The refusal |
| Verse 2 | 8 | Justification |
| Chorus | 8 | Repeat |
| Verse 3 | 8 | Defiance |
| Final Chorus | 8 | Resolution |

**Why this structure works:**
Repetitive chorus mirrors the repetitive refusal. No bridge = no compromise. The structure IS the stubbornness.

---

## Opening Technique

**Technique:** 1 (Dialogue Fragment)

**Opening line(s):**
> "They tried to make me go to rehab, I said no, no, no"

**Why it works:**
Reported speech and response in one line. We're immediately in a standoff.

---

## Hook Analysis

**The hook:**
> "No, no, no"

**Syllable count:** 3

**Conversational test:** Pass - It's literally just the word "no" repeated

**Why it works:**
- Shortest possible refusal
- Triple emphasis
- Defiant, stubborn, clear
- Musical as a melodic figure

---

## Notable Lyrical Techniques

### 1. Reasoning the unreasonable
**Example:**
> "I ain't got the time and if my daddy thinks I'm fine"

**Why it works:** Shows the denial logic

### 2. Humor in darkness
**Example:**
> "I'll be back to you in sixty-nine"

**Why it works:** Playful even in tragedy

### 3. Self-awareness without change
**Example:**
> She knows she drinks, won't stop

**Why it works:** Not deluded, just defiant

---

## "Said Not Written" Lines

Lines that sound like real speech:

1. > "I ain't got the time"
   - Sounds said because: Casual dismissal

2. > "If my daddy thinks I'm fine"
   - Sounds said because: Family dynamic in one line

3. > "I just think you're depressed"
   - Sounds said because: Her diagnosis of the situation

---

## Cliche Avoidance

**Cliches this song could have used but didn't:**

| Expected Cliche | What They Did Instead |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| "I have a problem" admission | Refused to admit |
| Recovery narrative | No recovery, just refusal |
| "I need help" | "I need no help from you" - specific |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Single word repeated ("no") can be entire hook
2. Defiance can be the whole song
3. Self-awareness without redemption is powerful
