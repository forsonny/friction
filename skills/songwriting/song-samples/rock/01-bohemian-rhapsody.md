# Bohemian Rhapsody

**Artist:** Queen
**Album:** A Night at the Opera (1975)
**Genre:** Progressive rock

---

## Structure Analysis

**Structure Type:** Unique - Opera format

| Section | Lines | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Ballad Intro | 8 | The confession |
| Ballad Verse | 8 | The murder |
| Guitar Solo | - | Transition |
| Opera Section | 16 | The trial |
| Hard Rock | 8 | The rage |
| Ballad Outro | 4 | Resolution |

**Why this structure works:**
Rules don't apply here. The structure is the content - a life unraveling through genres.

---

## Opening Technique

**Technique:** 4 (Rhetorical Question)

**Opening line(s):**
> "Is this the real life? Is this just fantasy?"

**Why it works:**
Existential question immediately. We're in philosophical crisis from word one.

---

## Hook Analysis

**The hook:**
> "Mama, just killed a man"

**Syllable count:** 6

**Conversational test:** Pass - Confession to a parent

**Why it works:**
- "Mama" is intimate, vulnerable
- "Just killed" is casual for murder
- Confession format
- The contrast is devastating

---

## Notable Lyrical Techniques

### 1. Opera within pop
**Example:**
> Galileo, Bismillah, Beelzebub

**Why it works:** Elevates pop to operatic stakes

### 2. Confession to mother
**Example:**
> "Mama" as repeated address

**Why it works:** Most intimate possible audience

### 3. Genre as emotion
**Example:**
> Soft for vulnerability, hard for rage

**Why it works:** Music changes with emotional state

## "Said Not Written" Lines

Lines that sound like real speech:

1. > "Mama, life had just begun"
   - Sounds said because: Child explaining to parent

2. > "Didn't mean to make you cry"
   - Sounds said because: Apology format

3. > "Nothing really matters to me"
   - Sounds said because: Nihilistic casual statement

## Cliche Avoidance

**Cliches this song could have used but didn't:**

| Expected Cliche | What They Did Instead |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| "I'm sorry" directly | Shows remorse through confession |
| Standard verse-chorus | Opera sections, genre changes |
| "I feel so bad" | Physical: "Put a gun against his head" |

## Key Takeaways

1. Structure can mirror emotional content
2. "Mama" is more devastating than "I"
3. Genre changes can represent emotional shifts
